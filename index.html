AI + Humanity = A Better Customer Experience?

This podcast episode from The WP Minute+ features Malcolm Peralty of Pressable discussing the intersection of AI and human elements in customer experience. Malcolm shares insights from his extensive WordPress and Drupal experience, highlighting how AI integrations can enhance website management while emphasizing the crucial role of human oversight in maintaining quality and trust. Key takeaways include the value of cross-CMS experience for innovation, the importance of transparent AI automation that reduces manual work without sacrificing user trust, and how technical account managers can guide client growth. The discussion also covers how combining AI insights with human judgment creates better customer experiences, and how diverse projects foster innovative problem-solving.

Interview with Sevalla

This interview explores Sevalla, a developer-first PaaS platform that was spun out from Kinsta to serve a different audience. While Kinsta remains focused on WordPress hosting excellence, Sevalla targets teams running custom applications, databases, and services who want production power without hyperscaler complexity. The name Sevalla follows Kinsta's Swedish village naming pattern, representing continuity while establishing a distinct identity. Key insights include how WordPress agencies can use Sevalla for non-WordPress projects like client portals, SaaS products, and custom applications without managing Kubernetes or cloud infrastructure. The platform offers clear support boundaries focused on infrastructure issues rather than application debugging, and provides agency programs for predictable pricing. The interview emphasizes that separating the brands allows both platforms to evolve independently while serving their respective audiences more effectively.

What Happens to Your Agency When You’re Sick?

This episode of The WP Minute's Agency Action podcast features Kurt Von Ahnen and Toby Cryns discussing critical but often overlooked aspects of running a WordPress agency. The conversation centers on planning for health emergencies and business continuity, emphasizing the importance of long-term disability and health insurance to protect the agency when the owner is sick or incapacitated. The hosts also cover practical business strategies including effective pricing to avoid race-to-the-bottom competition, lead generation through organic social media and referrals, client screening to identify red flags, and building long-term client relationships. Additional insights include the psychology behind risk reversal offers like 30-day money-back guarantees, niche specialization for agency success, and stress management techniques like journaling for maintaining work-life balance.
