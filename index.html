How WordPress Is Reaching the Next Generation of Contributors

This article discusses the WordPress Credits Program, an initiative led by Isotta Peira that connects students with the open-source WordPress community. The program provides practical WordPress skills through mentorship, comprehensive learning experiences, and culminates in certificates of completion, welcoming students from diverse academic backgrounds. The initiative includes the Campus Connect program that brings WordPress education to universities worldwide, focusing on building personal websites and teaching open-source contributions. Key elements include mentorship support, empowering teachers, and encouraging students to continue as active community members and future mentors, with the goal of expanding the program's impact on students' careers and open-source contributions.

Can Elementor One Accelerate the Page Builder's Growth?

This article features an interview with Gabriella Laster, Director of Product Marketing at Elementor, discussing the launch of Elementor One. The conversation covers how Elementor One consolidates multiple features into a single package with a flexible credit system, the importance of user feedback in product development, and the challenges of balancing technical features with user experience. Key insights include the integration of AI into Elementor's offerings, the philosophical implications of AI in WordPress, and how AI can empower users to create customized websites. The discussion also emphasizes the need for clear communication with users and ongoing education about technical aspects to ensure user success, highlighting Elementor's focus on AI integration as a significant part of their future strategy.

Why Your Agency Should Be About More Than Money

This article explores the responsibilities of agency owners beyond revenue generation, emphasizing the obligation to give back to the WordPress community and society. The discussion covers contributions through meetups, WordCamps, and Five for the Future-style initiatives, as well as investing in local neighborhoods, mentoring the next generation, and addressing ageism in tech. Key takeaways include building trust through honest feedback and proactive leadership, avoiding the 'race to the bottom' by selling expertise rather than just services, and fostering long-term relationships through stability and calm communication. The core message is that agencies built on open-source software have a natural duty to contribute through time, money, mentorship, and community engagement.
