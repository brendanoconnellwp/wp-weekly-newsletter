Show Airtable Data in Bricks’ Query Loops with Bricksforge API Builder

The article introduces the Bricks Forge API Query Builder, a feature enabling WordPress developers to integrate external data sources like Airtable into their sites without importing the data into the WordPress database. It provides a step-by-step guide on setting up the Airtable integration, including authentication, creating API endpoints, fetching data, and displaying it dynamically on the frontend. The feature also supports sorting, filtering, and adding images to enhance the presentation of external content.

Accepting Change in WordPress

The article discusses the evolving landscape of WordPress and the broader tech industry, focusing on AI's impact on development, podcasting, and content creation. Matt and Nathan Wrigley explore how AI tools like ChatGPT and Repl.it are transforming workflows, enabling faster prototyping, and raising ethical concerns. They highlight the need for WordPress professionals to adapt by integrating AI, leveraging headless WordPress, and embracing automation. The conversation also touches on the shift toward video-based podcasting and the importance of storytelling in engaging audiences. The future of AI remains uncertain, with questions about sustainability and economic impact.

Why ‘Boring’ Features Benefit WordPress Professionals

The article highlights the value of under-the-hood, incremental improvements in WordPress, often overshadowed by flashy new features. It discusses how changes like bcrypt password encryption in WordPress 6.8 enhance security and stability without fanfare. The piece also reflects on the Automattic vs. WP Engine saga, noting how reduced contributor hours have shifted focus toward polishing existing features and fixing bugs. This shift, while less glamorous, benefits professionals by reducing the learning curve and improving reliability, making WordPress more stable and efficient for long-term use.
